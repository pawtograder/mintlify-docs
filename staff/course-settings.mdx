# Course Settings

Configure course-wide settings that affect how Pawtograder displays information and manages policies for all students in your course.

## Time Zone Configuration

Pawtograder allows you to configure the time zone used for displaying dates and times throughout your course. This ensures that all due dates, timestamps, and time-based features display in your local time zone.

### Setting Your Course Time Zone

1. Navigate to **Course Settings** in the course navigation menu
2. Find the **Time Zone** setting
3. Select your preferred time zone from the dropdown menu
4. Click **Save** to apply the changes

### Default Time Zone

If no time zone is configured, Pawtograder defaults to **America/New_York** (Eastern Time).

### What Time Zone Affects

The configured time zone affects:

- **Assignment due dates**: Students see deadlines in the course's configured time zone
- **Survey due dates**: Survey deadlines display in the configured time zone
- **Poll close times**: Automatic poll deactivation times use the configured time zone
- **Submission timestamps**: When students submit work, timestamps display in the configured time zone
- **Gradebook timestamps**: Import and modification times display in the configured time zone
- **Response timestamps**: Survey and poll response times display in the configured time zone

<Note>
The time zone setting is course-wide and applies to all students and staff in the course. Individual users cannot override this setting.
</Note>

<Tip>
Set your time zone to match your institution's location or the primary location where your course is taught. This ensures that due dates and times are intuitive for your students.
</Tip>

## Late Token Policy

Configure the class-wide late token policy that determines how many late tokens students receive and how they can be used.

### Configuring Late Tokens

1. Navigate to **Course Settings**
2. Find the **Late Tokens** section
3. Set the number of late tokens available to each student
4. Configure whether tokens can be used on specific assignments (set per-assignment)

See [Due Date Exceptions](/staff/assignments/intro#due-date-exceptions) for more details on late token configuration.

## Grading Conflicts

Manage conflicts of interest to ensure graders do not grade assignments where they have a conflict.

Graders can self-input their conflicts under **Course Settings** → **Grading Conflicts**, and instructors can review and enter conflicts for all graders in the class.

See [Grading Assignments](/staff/assignments/grading-assignments) for more details on managing grading conflicts.
