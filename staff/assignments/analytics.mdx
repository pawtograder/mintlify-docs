# Assignment Repository Analytics

Pawtograder provides comprehensive analytics for assignment repositories, allowing instructors to monitor student engagement, track commit activity, and identify students who may need additional support.

## Accessing Repository Analytics

Navigate to an assignment and select the **Analytics** tab to view repository activity metrics for all students.

## Available Metrics

### Commit Activity

Track the number of commits made by each student or group to their assignment repository. This helps identify:

- Students who are actively working on the assignment
- Students who may have started late or need encouragement
- Patterns of work distribution in group assignments

### Repository Access Patterns

Monitor when students are accessing and working on their repositories to understand:

- Peak activity times
- Last activity timestamps
- Overall engagement levels

### Group Activity Distribution

For group assignments, analytics show individual contribution patterns within each group, helping instructors:

- Identify uneven workload distribution
- Recognize students who may not be participating
- Facilitate conversations about team dynamics

## Using Analytics for Student Support

Repository analytics can help instructors proactively support students by:

- **Early intervention**: Identify students with low activity before the deadline
- **Office hours planning**: Reach out to students who appear stuck or inactive
- **Group dynamics**: Address imbalances in group work contributions
- **Deadline extensions**: Make informed decisions about extension requests based on activity patterns

<Tip>
Combine repository analytics with autograder results to get a complete picture of student progress. High commit activity with low test pass rates may indicate students who are struggling and need help.
</Tip>
