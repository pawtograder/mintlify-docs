# Grading Status Dashboard

The grading status dashboard provides a comprehensive view of grading progress for an assignment (and across all assignments in your course), helping you track which submissions have been graded, manage grader assignments, identify bottlenecks, and ensure timely completion of grading tasks.

## Accessing the Dashboard

- **Per assignment**: Navigate to an assignment and select the **Grading Status** tab, or view the grading status overview on the assignment's management page.
- **Across the course**: Navigate to the **Grading Status** section from the course navigation menu.

The dashboard is available to instructors and head TAs.

## Dashboard Overview

The dashboard displays:

- **Total submissions**: Number of student submissions for the assignment.
- **Graded / pending submissions**: How many have been fully graded and how many remain.
- **Grading progress**: A visual progress bar showing the percentage of submissions graded.
- **Grader assignments**: Which staff members are assigned to which submissions.
- **Completion timeline**: When grading tasks were completed.
- **Average grading time**: How long graders are taking per submission.

For a course-wide view, each assignment row shows the assignment name and due date, total submissions received, grading progress, ungraded submission count, and assigned graders.

## Grading Progress Tracking

### Overall progress

- **Completion percentage**: Visual progress bar showing grading completion.
- **Submissions by status**: Breakdown of graded vs. pending submissions.
- **Estimated time to completion**: Projected time to finish all pending submissions based on the current pace.

### Per-grader statistics

- **Assigned submissions**: How many submissions each grader has.
- **Completed submissions**: How many they've finished grading.
- **Pending submissions**: What's still in their queue.
- **Average time per submission**: Grading efficiency metrics.
- Overdue assignments are highlighted in red.

## Grading States

Submissions can be in one of several states:

- **Not started**: No grading has been completed.
- **In progress**: Some rubric items have been graded, but the review is not complete.
- **Completed**: All required rubric items have been graded (but not necessarily released).
- **Released**: Grades have been released to students.

<Note>
Completing a review does not automatically release it to students. Instructors must explicitly release grades when ready.
</Note>

## Managing Grader Assignments

### Assigning graders

You can assign graders to submissions in two ways:

- **Bulk assignment**: Assign multiple submissions to a grader at once.
- **Individual assignment**: Assign specific submissions to specific graders.

### Partial rubric grading

Instructors can assign graders to specific parts of the rubric rather than entire submissions:

- **Full rubric assignment**: Grader handles all rubric items for a submission.
- **Partial assignment**: Grader only grades specific rubric parts.

This allows you to distribute grading work more efficiently and leverage grader expertise.

### Reassigning submissions

If a grader is overloaded or unavailable:

1. Select the submissions to reassign.
2. Choose a new grader from the dropdown.
3. Click **Reassign** to update the assignments.

See [Assigning Grading Tasks](/staff/assignments/grading-assignments) for more details on reassignment.

## Taking Action

From the grading status dashboard, you can:

- **Reassign tasks**: Click a grader to view their assignments and reassign if needed.
- **Send reminders**: Notify graders about pending assignments approaching their due dates.
- **View submissions**: Click any assignment to jump directly to the grading interface.
- **Adjust deadlines**: Extend grading deadlines for assignments that are behind schedule.

## Filtering and Sorting

Use the filters at the top of the dashboard to:

- Show only assignments with pending grading tasks
- Filter by assignment due date or grading deadline
- Sort by grading progress (least to most complete)
- Filter by specific graders or sections

## Grading Metrics

### Quality metrics

- **Score distribution**: How grades are distributed across submissions.
- **Rubric item usage**: Which rubric items are most commonly applied.
- **Feedback length**: Average length of grader comments.

## Grading Notifications

Graders receive notifications when:

- New submissions are assigned to them
- Submission deadlines are approaching
- Students submit regrade requests

Configure notification preferences in your user settings.

## Best Practices

- **Balance workload**: Distribute submissions evenly among graders and use the grader breakdown to identify overloaded graders.
- **Leverage expertise**: Consider grader expertise when assigning specific rubric parts.
- **Set realistic deadlines**: Allow sufficient time between assignment due dates and grade release dates.
- **Check regularly**: Review the dashboard weekly during the grading period to ensure grading stays on track.
- **Address bottlenecks early**: Reassign tasks or recruit additional graders when progress falls behind.
- **Review quality**: Review a sample of each grader's work to ensure consistent application of rubric criteria, and provide feedback to graders on grading quality.
- **Communicate**: Keep graders informed of deadlines, share common issues or clarifications, and recognize graders who complete work efficiently.

<Tip>
Check the grading status dashboard regularly during the grading period to ensure all submissions are being processed on time.
</Tip>
