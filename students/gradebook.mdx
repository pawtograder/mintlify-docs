# Student Gradebook

The student gradebook shows your released grades and allows you to explore hypothetical scenarios with What-If calculations. Your instructor controls which grades are visible to you by releasing individual gradebook columns.

## Viewing Your Grades

Navigate to the **Gradebook** page from the course menu to see all released grades. Grades are organized by category, and you can use the **Expand All** and **Collapse All** buttons to show or hide all groups at once.

Each grade card displays:

- **Column name**: The assignment or grade component (e.g., "Homework 1", "Final Grade")
- **Your score**: Your current score out of the maximum points possible
- **Percentage earned**: Your score as a percentage of the maximum
- **Status indicators**: Visual cues for missing, excused, incomplete, or calculated values
- **Magic wand icon**: Indicates an active What-If simulation
- **View Submission link**: Opens the related assignment submission (if applicable)

For calculated columns (those using expressions), you see the current calculation based only on the grades that have been released to you. For example, if your instructor has a "Homework Average" column that averages all homework assignments, you'll only see the average of the homework assignments that have been released.

Grades are only visible after your instructor releases them. Unreleased columns do not appear in your gradebook.

### Expand and Collapse Groups

When multiple related columns are grouped together (e.g., "Homework 1", "Homework 2", "Homework 3"), you can:

- **Expand All**: Show all individual columns
- **Collapse All**: Show only the most recent or relevant column per group

This keeps your gradebook organized when you have many assignments.

## What-If Calculations

<Note>
What-If simulations must be enabled by your instructor. If disabled, you can still view released grades but cannot edit them, and you'll see a notice at the top of the gradebook.
</Note>

What-If mode lets you simulate hypothetical grades to see how they would affect your calculated grades and overall course grade. This helps you:

- Understand how future assignments impact your final grade
- Plan which assignments to prioritize
- Explore grade scenarios without affecting your actual grades

### Using What-If Mode

1. **Click a grade card** to edit it (only manual columns and assignment-linked grades can be edited)
2. **Enter a hypothetical score** in the input field
3. **Press Enter** to apply the simulation
4. **View updated calculations** in dependent columns (e.g., averages, final grade)

Cards with active simulations are highlighted with a magic wand icon to distinguish them from actual grades. Any calculated columns that depend on the grade you changed will automatically update to show what your grade would be.

### Clearing Simulations

To reset a What-If value and return to your actual grade:

1. Click the grade card with the simulation
2. Clear the input field (delete all text)
3. Press Enter

The grade returns to your actual released score, and all dependent calculated columns automatically update to reflect your actual grades.

### Read-Only Columns

Calculated columns (those with formulas) cannot be directly edited. They automatically update when you change the manual grades they depend on.

### Limitations

- You can only simulate grades for **manual columns** and **assignment-linked grades**
- **Calculated columns** (averages, weighted totals, final grades) are read-only and update automatically based on your simulations
- What-If values are **not saved** between sessions and are only visible to you
- Simulations do not affect your actual grades or what your instructor sees
- You can only simulate grades for **released columns**
- Columns with instructor overrides cannot be simulated
- If What-If is disabled for your course, the gradebook is view-only

<Note>
What-If simulations are a planning tool only. They do not change your actual grades or notify your instructor.
</Note>

## Understanding Incomplete Values

Some calculated grades may show warnings about incomplete or unreleased dependencies. This means:

- **Missing dependencies**: Some grades that feed into the calculation haven't been entered yet
- **Unreleased dependencies**: Some grades that feed into the calculation haven't been released by your instructor yet

These warnings help you understand why a calculated grade might be lower than expected or why it might change when more grades are released.
