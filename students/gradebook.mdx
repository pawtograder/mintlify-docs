# Student Gradebook

The gradebook shows your current grades for all released assignments and grade columns in the course.

## Viewing Your Grades

Your instructor controls which grades are visible to you. You'll see:

- **Released grades**: Grades that your instructor has made visible
- **Calculated columns**: Automatically computed grades based on released dependencies
- **Assignment scores**: Grades from programming assignments and other coursework

Grades are organized into collapsible groups. Use the "Expand All" and "Collapse All" buttons to show or hide all groups at once.

## What-If Grade Simulations

If your instructor has enabled the "What-If" feature, you can simulate hypothetical grades to see how they would affect your overall course grade.

### How to Use What-If

1. **Click on a grade card** for a manually-entered grade or assignment
2. **Enter a hypothetical score** in the input field that appears
3. **Press Enter** to apply the simulation
4. **View updated calculations**: Any calculated columns that depend on the grade you changed will automatically update to show what your grade would be

### Clearing Simulations

To remove a what-if simulation and return to your actual grade:

1. Click on the grade card with the simulation
2. Clear the input field (delete all text)
3. Press Enter

All dependent calculated columns will return to showing your actual grades.

### Limitations

- You can only simulate grades for manually-entered columns and assignments
- You cannot directly edit calculated columns (they update automatically based on your simulations)
- What-if simulations are only visible to you and do not affect your actual grades
- If the feature is disabled, the gradebook is view-only

<Note>
What-if simulations are a planning tool only. They do not change your actual grades or notify your instructor.
</Note>

# Gradebook

The student gradebook shows your released grades for the course. Your instructor controls which grades are visible to you by releasing individual gradebook columns.

## Viewing your grades

Navigate to the Gradebook page from the course menu to see all released grades. Grades are organized by category, and you can expand or collapse groups to focus on specific assignments or grade components.

Each grade card shows:
- Your score out of the maximum points
- The percentage earned
- Any incomplete values or missing dependencies (for calculated columns)

## What-If grade simulations

If your instructor has enabled the "What-If" feature for your course, you can simulate hypothetical scores to see how they would affect your overall grade.

### How to use What-If

1. Click on any released grade card that allows editing (manual grades and assignment scores)
2. Enter a hypothetical score in the input field
3. Press Enter to apply the simulation
4. Calculated columns (like averages and final grades) will automatically update based on your simulated scores

The What-If simulation shows you a preview of how different scores would affect your grade, but it doesn't change your actual grades in the system. Your instructor still sees your real grades.

The student gradebook shows your grades for all released gradebook columns in your course. Your instructor controls which columns are visible to you and when they are released.

## Viewing Your Grades

The gradebook displays your grades organized by category or assignment type. Each grade card shows:

- **Column name**: The name of the grade component (e.g., "Homework 1", "Final Grade")
- **Your score**: Your current score out of the maximum points
- **Status indicators**: Visual cues for missing grades, incomplete dependencies, or calculated values

For calculated columns (those using expressions), you see the current calculation based only on the grades that have been released to you. For example, if your instructor has a "Homework Average" column that averages all homework assignments, you'll only see the average of the homework assignments that have been released.

## What If Calculations

If your instructor has enabled the "What If" feature for your course, you can simulate hypothetical scores to see how they would affect your calculated grades.

### Using What If

To simulate a grade:

1. Click on any released grade card that allows editing (manual grades and assignment-linked grades)
2. Enter a hypothetical score in the input field
3. Press Enter to apply the simulation

When you enter a What If value:

- The grade card displays your simulated score with a magic wand icon
- All calculated columns that depend on that grade automatically update to show what your grade would be with the simulated value
- Your actual grades in the system are not changed - this is only a simulation

### Clearing Simulations

To remove a What If simulation:
1. Click on the grade card with the simulated value
2. Clear the input field (delete all text)
3. Press Enter

All dependent calculated columns will automatically recalculate based on your actual grades.

### Limitations

- You can only simulate scores for manual columns and assignment grades
- Calculated columns (like averages) are read-only and update automatically based on your simulations
- What-If simulations are only visible to you and don't affect your actual grades
- If What-If is disabled for your course, you can still view released grades but cannot simulate scores

The grade card will return to showing your actual grade, and all dependent calculated columns will revert to their actual values.

### Limitations

- You can only simulate grades for manual columns and assignment-linked columns
- Calculated columns (those using expressions) cannot be directly edited - they update automatically based on your simulated values
- What If simulations are only visible to you and do not affect your actual grades or what your instructor sees
- If What If is disabled for your course, the gradebook is view-only and you'll see a notice at the top

## Understanding Incomplete Values

Some calculated grades may show warnings about incomplete or unreleased dependencies. This means:

- **Missing dependencies**: Some grades that feed into the calculation haven't been entered yet
- **Unreleased dependencies**: Some grades that feed into the calculation haven't been released by your instructor yet

These warnings help you understand why a calculated grade might be lower than expected or why it might change when more grades are released.
