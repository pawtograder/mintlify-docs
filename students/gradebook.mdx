# Student Gradebook

The student gradebook shows your released grades and allows you to explore hypothetical scenarios with What-If calculations.

## Viewing Your Grades

Your gradebook displays all released grade columns organized by category. Each grade card shows:

- **Column name**: The assignment or grade component
- **Your score**: Current grade (e.g., "85/100")
- **Status indicators**: Missing, excused, or incomplete grades
- **Submission links**: Quick access to view your submission

Grades are only visible after your instructor releases them. Unreleased columns do not appear in your gradebook.

## What-If Calculations

<Note>
What-If simulations must be enabled by your instructor. If disabled, you can still view released grades but cannot edit them.
</Note>

What-If mode allows you to simulate hypothetical grades and see how they would affect your overall course grade. This helps you:

- Understand how future assignments impact your final grade
- Plan which assignments to prioritize
- Explore grade scenarios without affecting your actual grades

### Using What-If Mode

1. **Click a grade card** to edit it (only manual columns can be edited)
2. **Enter a hypothetical score** in the input field
3. **Press Enter** to apply the simulation
4. **View updated calculations** in dependent columns (e.g., final grade)

Calculated columns (like averages or weighted totals) automatically update based on your simulated scores.

### Clearing Simulations

To reset a What-If value:

1. Click the grade card
2. Clear the input field (delete all text)
3. Press Enter

The grade returns to your actual released score, and all dependent calculations update accordingly.

### Read-Only Columns

Calculated columns (those with formulas) cannot be directly edited. They automatically update when you change the manual grades they depend on.

### Limitations

- What-If values are **not saved** between sessions
- Simulations only affect your view—instructors see your actual grades
- You can only simulate grades for released columns
- Columns with instructor overrides cannot be simulated

## Grade Cards

Each grade card displays:

- **Score**: Your current or simulated grade
- **Maximum score**: Total points possible
- **Magic wand icon**: Indicates a What-If simulation is active
- **View Submission link**: Opens the related assignment submission (if applicable)

Cards with active simulations are highlighted to distinguish them from actual grades.

## Incomplete Values

If a calculated column depends on unreleased or missing grades, you'll see an alert explaining which dependencies are incomplete. This helps you understand why a calculated grade might be lower than expected.

## Expand/Collapse Groups

When multiple related columns are grouped together (e.g., "Homework 1", "Homework 2", "Homework 3"), you can:

- **Expand All**: Show all individual columns
- **Collapse All**: Show only the most recent or relevant column per group

This keeps your gradebook organized when you have many assignments.
