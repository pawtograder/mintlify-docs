# What-if grade simulations

The gradebook what-if feature lets you simulate hypothetical grades to see how they would affect your calculated grades and final course grade.

<Note>
This feature must be enabled by your instructor. If you don't see edit options on your grades, what-if simulations are not available for your course.
</Note>

## How it works

When what-if simulations are enabled, you can click on any released grade to enter a hypothetical score. Calculated columns (like averages and final grades) automatically update to show what your grade would be with that change.

### Simulating a grade

1. Navigate to your gradebook
2. Click on a grade card for a manual column (assignments, participation, etc.)
3. Enter a hypothetical score in the input field
4. Press Enter or click outside the input

The grade card will show your simulated score with a magic wand icon, and any calculated columns that depend on it will update automatically.

### Clearing a simulation

To remove a what-if value and return to your actual grade:

1. Click on the grade card showing the simulation
2. Clear the input field (delete all text)
3. Press Enter or click outside the input

All dependent calculated columns will return to showing your actual grades.

## What you can simulate

- **Manual grades**: Assignments, participation, and other instructor-entered grades
- **Imported grades**: Grades imported from CSV files

## What you cannot simulate

- **Calculated columns**: Columns with expressions (averages, totals, etc.) are read-only
- **Overridden grades**: Grades with instructor overrides cannot be simulated

## Understanding the display

- **Normal grade**: Shows your actual score (e.g., "85/100")
- **Simulated grade**: Shows with a magic wand icon and different styling
- **Calculated update**: Dependent columns update automatically to reflect your simulation

## Tips

- Simulations are temporary and only visible to you
- Your actual grades are never changed by what-if simulations
- Simulations reset when you refresh the page
- Use what-if to explore how upcoming assignments affect your final grade
