# What-If Grade Simulations

The What-If feature allows you to simulate hypothetical grades to see how they would affect your calculated grades and final course grade.

<Note>
This feature must be enabled by your instructor. If you don't see edit options on your gradebook, What-If simulations are not available for your course.
</Note>

## How It Works

When What-If is enabled, you can tap any released grade card to enter a hypothetical score. Calculated columns (like averages or final grades) will automatically update to show what your grade would be with that change.

### Editing a Grade

1. Navigate to your gradebook
2. Tap on any released grade card
3. Enter a hypothetical score
4. Press Enter to apply the simulation

The card will show your simulated score with a magic wand icon to indicate it's a what-if value.

### Cascading Calculations

When you simulate a grade, all calculated columns that depend on it will automatically update. For example:

- If you simulate a higher score on Homework 1, your "Homework Average" will update
- If your "Final Grade" depends on "Homework Average", it will also update to reflect the simulation

### Clearing Simulations

To remove a what-if simulation and return to your actual grade:

1. Tap the grade card again
2. Clear the input field (delete all text)
3. Press Enter

All dependent calculated columns will automatically revert to their actual values.

## Limitations

- You can only simulate grades for **manually-entered columns** (assignments, participation, etc.)
- **Calculated columns** (averages, final grades) are read-only and update automatically based on your simulations
- Simulations are **local to your browser** and don't affect your actual grades
- Your instructor cannot see your what-if simulations
