# Discussion Board

Pawtograder's discussion board feature allows students and staff to discuss course content and ask questions. It is **not** intended to be a replacement for office hours, although there is an option to format the post as a question that needs an answer (as opposed to a "note" that does not need an answer). 

Posts are formatted as Markdown, and support drag-and-drop attachments and images. Simply drag an image file directly into the editor to upload and embed it in your post.

Posts are formatted as Markdown and support:
- Drag-and-drop attachments
- Drag-and-drop image uploads for easy visual sharing
- Code blocks and formatting

Posts are formatted as Markdown, and support drag-and-drop attachments and images. You can drag image files directly into the post editor to upload them, making it easy to share screenshots, diagrams, or other visual content.

## Adding Images and Attachments

You can easily add images to your posts and replies using drag-and-drop:

1. **Drag and drop**: Simply drag an image file from your computer directly into the Markdown editor
2. **Automatic upload**: The image is automatically uploaded and embedded in your post
3. **Markdown formatting**: The editor inserts the proper Markdown syntax for the image

This makes it easy to share screenshots, diagrams, error messages, and other visual content with your classmates and instructors.

### Feed Navigation

- **My Feed**: Your personalized view of posts, showing content from topics you follow and posts you've interacted with
- **Browse Topics**: Explore all discussion topics organized by category
- **Followed topics**: Filter to show only posts from categories you're following

### Pinned Posts

The sidebar displays **Pinned Posts**: important threads highlighted by your instructors. These typically include:

- Frequently asked questions
- Important announcements
- Helpful resources and guides

Pinned posts stay visible in the sidebar for quick access, regardless of how many new posts are created.

### Error Pins

When you encounter an error in your assignment submission, Pawtograder may automatically suggest relevant discussion posts that could help you resolve the issue. These "error pins" appear directly on your submission page, showing:

- Discussion posts related to the specific error you encountered
- Posts that other students found helpful for similar issues
- Instructor-highlighted solutions for common problems

This feature helps you find answers quickly without having to search through the entire discussion board.

### Top Posts This Week

The sidebar also shows **Top Posts This Week**, highlighting the most engaging discussions based on community activity.

## Get Help Button

A persistent **Get Help** button appears in the bottom-right corner of the page, providing quick access to office hours. Click it to:

- View the help drawer with office hours status
- See available queues and their current activity
- Submit a new help request
- Check the weekly queue schedule

The button includes notifications to alert you of important updates, ensuring you stay connected to office hours support.

### Leaderboard

If enabled by your instructor, the sidebar displays a **Leaderboard** showing top contributors based on karma points. Karma is earned through helpful contributions to the discussion board, such as:

- Creating posts that receive likes
- Providing answers that are marked as helpful
- Engaging constructively with the community

The leaderboard encourages positive participation and recognizes students who actively help their peers.

## Active Help Request Widget

When you have an active help request in office hours, it follows you around the site! A widget appears in the sidebar showing:

- Your current position in the queue
- Status updates (e.g., "Staff is here helping you now!")
- Video call notifications when a TA starts a video chat
- A **Join Video Call** button for instant access

### Notifications

Pawtograder provides rich notifications for office hours and messages:

- **Browser notifications**: Desktop alerts when you receive messages or status updates
- **Title notifications**: Unread message count in your browser tab title
- **Sound alerts**: Audio notifications for new messages (can be tested in settings)
- **Favicon badge**: Visual indicator of unread messages

You can test notification settings and grant browser permissions from the help drawer.

## Post Visibility and Identity

Posts can be shared with the entire class, or with course staff only. Each user in a class has a pseudonym, and can choose to use their real name or their pseudonym. Staff can always see the real name of a user.

Students can edit their own posts, and staff can edit any post.

## Notifications

By default, users do not get notifications for new posts, but can optionally "watch" a post to receive notifications when the post is updated or receives a new reply. Interacting with a post (e.g. replying to it) will automatically watch the post.

## Creating a New Post

Click the **+ New Post** button in the top right to create a new discussion thread. When creating a post:

1. **Select a topic**: Choose a category to help organize your post
2. **Choose visibility**: Share with the entire class or staff only
3. **Select identity**: Post with your real name or pseudonym
4. **Mark as question or note**: Questions indicate you need an answer; notes are for sharing information
5. **Add images**: Drag and drop images directly into the editor, or paste them from your clipboard for quick sharing

5. **Add content**: Write your post using Markdown formatting
6. **Attach files**: Drag and drop images directly into your post for easy sharing

5. **Add images**: Drag and drop images directly into the editor to upload them inline with your content

The system prevents accidental double-posting by disabling the submit button after you click it. If you need to make changes after submitting, you can edit your post using the edit button.

## Engaging with Posts

Each post displays helpful metadata:

- **Topic tag**: Color-coded category (e.g., "Logistics", "Assignments")
- **Author**: Who created the post
- **Timestamp**: When it was posted
- **Replies**: Number of responses, with "(X new)" indicators for unread replies
- **Likes**: Community engagement—click the heart to show appreciation
- **Karma**: Earn karma points for helpful contributions to the discussion board
- **Answered badge**: Indicates questions that have been resolved

Replies are threaded, allowing for organized conversations. Use the Markdown editor to format your responses with code blocks, links, images, and more.

## Error Pins

When you encounter an error in your submission, Pawtograder may display "Error Pins" - suggested discussion posts that are relevant to your specific error. This feature helps you:

- Find existing solutions to common errors
- Connect with other students who encountered similar issues
- Get help faster by seeing relevant discussions

Error pins appear automatically when the system detects patterns in your autograder results that match existing discussion threads. This integration between the autograder and discussion board helps you troubleshoot issues more efficiently.
