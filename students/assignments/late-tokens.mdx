# Late Tokens

Pawtograder supports late submission policies based on "late tokens." Instructors determine how many late tokens are available to each student, and how many tokens can be used for each late submission. Each token extends the deadline by 24 hours, with no penalty.

<Warning>
Late tokens must be applied **before** the deadline. If multiple late tokens are permitted, you can apply them all at once or one at a time, but you must always apply them before the updated deadline.
</Warning>

## Applying Late Tokens

How you apply late tokens depends on the policy your instructor has configured for the assignment.

### Apply before the deadline (default)

By default, you must apply late tokens **before** the original due date passes. On each assignment page where late tokens can be applied, you'll see an **Extend Due Date** button next to your due date. A note below the button reminds you that tokens must be applied before the due date.

To use multiple late tokens, click the **Extend Due Date** button again.

### Auto-apply after the deadline

Some instructors allow late tokens to be applied automatically when you submit after the deadline. When this policy is in effect:

- The assignment page shows the message: *"Deadline passed: submitting will auto-apply a late token if you have one remaining."*
- You can still use the **Extend Due Date** button to apply tokens manually before the deadline if you prefer.
- When you push a submission after the deadline, Pawtograder automatically calculates how many tokens are needed and deducts them from your balance. If you don't have enough tokens, your submission will be rejected with a message explaining how many tokens are needed.

<Warning>
Even with auto-apply enabled, you must have enough remaining tokens to cover the time you are late. If you run out of tokens, you will not be able to submit.
</Warning>

## Gifted Late Tokens

Instructors can gift additional late tokens to individual students beyond the class-wide allocation, on a case-by-case basis. Gifted tokens:

- Are automatically added to your available token balance
- Work exactly like your original tokens—each extends a deadline by 24 hours and must be applied before your current due date
- Display in your remaining token count (which always reflects both your initial tokens and any gifted tokens)
- Can be used even if you've exhausted your initial allocation
- Are tracked separately in the system (they appear as negative values internally) for instructor visibility

For example, if you've used all 4 of your initial tokens but your instructor gifts you 1 additional token, you'll see 1 remaining token available to use.

## Late Tokens and Group Assignments

The semantics of late tokens for group assignments are intended to be as straightforward and fair as possible:

- Any student in the group can apply a late token as long as they have one available (and the maximum number of tokens hasn't yet been applied to the assignment). It does not matter if a groupmate is out of tokens.
- All group members will have a token deducted from their total.
- All group members will see the same due date.
