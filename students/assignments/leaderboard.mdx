# Assignment Leaderboard

Some assignments may include an optional leaderboard feature that displays student rankings based on assignment scores. Leaderboards add a competitive element to assignments and can motivate students to optimize their solutions, while maintaining privacy options.

<Note>
Leaderboards are optional and must be enabled by your instructor for each assignment. Not all assignments will have leaderboards.
</Note>

## Viewing the Leaderboard

If your instructor has enabled the leaderboard for an assignment, you'll see a **Leaderboard** tab on the assignment page. The leaderboard displays:

- **Rankings**: Your position relative to other students, with your current rank highlighted
- **Scores**: Current assignment scores (typically based on autograder results)
- **Anonymity options**: Depending on instructor settings, leaderboards may show real names or pseudonyms

## How Rankings Work

Leaderboard rankings are based on:

1. **Autograder scores**: Your active submission's autograder score
2. **Submission time**: In case of ties, earlier submissions rank higher
3. **Completion status**: Whether the submission is complete (if configured by your instructor)

Rankings update automatically as students submit new code.

## Leaderboard Privacy

The leaderboard is designed to be optional and privacy-conscious. Instructors can configure leaderboard privacy settings:

- **Public leaderboards**: All students can see everyone's rankings
- **Anonymous leaderboards**: Students see rankings but not identities
- **Personal view only**: You only see your own rank and score

Students can choose whether to display their name or remain anonymous. The leaderboard is intentionally less prominent in the interface to avoid creating pressure, and participation in the competitive aspect is entirely voluntary.

## Competitive Assignments

Leaderboards are particularly useful for:

- **Optimization challenges**: Assignments where students compete for the fastest or most efficient solution
- **Performance tasks**: Problems where there are multiple correct approaches with varying quality
- **Extra credit opportunities**: Bonus points for top performers

<Note>
The leaderboard is meant to be a fun, optional feature. Your grade is not affected by your leaderboard position—it's purely for those who enjoy a competitive element.
</Note>

<Tip>
Focus on learning and understanding the material first. Leaderboards are meant to be fun and motivating, not stressful!
</Tip>

## Opting Out

If you prefer not to participate in leaderboards, check with your instructor about opt-out options. Some courses allow students to hide their names from public leaderboards while still tracking their own progress.

## Instructor Configuration

Instructors control whether the leaderboard appears for each assignment. If you don't see a **Leaderboard** tab, your instructor has chosen not to enable this feature for the current assignment.
