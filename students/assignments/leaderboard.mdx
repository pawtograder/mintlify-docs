# Assignment Leaderboard

Some assignments may include an optional leaderboard feature that displays student rankings based on assignment scores. The leaderboard adds a competitive element to assignments while maintaining privacy options.

## Viewing the Leaderboard

If your instructor has enabled the leaderboard for an assignment, you'll see a "Leaderboard" tab on the assignment page.

The leaderboard displays:
- **Rankings**: Your position relative to other students
- **Scores**: Current assignment scores (may be based on autograder results)
- **Anonymity options**: Students can choose to appear anonymously or with their name

## Privacy and Participation

The leaderboard is designed to be optional and privacy-conscious:

- Instructors can enable or disable the leaderboard per assignment
- Students can choose whether to display their name or remain anonymous
- The leaderboard is less prominent in the interface to avoid creating pressure
- Participation in the competitive aspect is entirely voluntary

## How Rankings Work

Rankings are typically based on:
- Autograder scores
- Submission timing (if configured by instructor)
- Completion status

<Note>
The leaderboard is meant to be a fun, optional feature. Your grade is not affected by your leaderboard position—it's purely for those who enjoy a competitive element.
</Note>

## Instructor Configuration

Instructors control whether the leaderboard appears for each assignment. If you don't see a leaderboard tab, your instructor has chosen not to enable this feature for the current assignment.

Some assignments may include an optional leaderboard feature that displays student rankings based on their autograder scores. Leaderboards add a competitive element to assignments and can motivate students to optimize their solutions.

<Note>
Leaderboards are optional and must be enabled by your instructor for each assignment. Not all assignments will have leaderboards.
</Note>

## Viewing the Leaderboard

If an assignment has a leaderboard enabled, you'll see a **Leaderboard** tab on the assignment page. The leaderboard displays:

- **Rankings**: Your position relative to other students
- **Scores**: Autograder scores for each student
- **Anonymity options**: Depending on instructor settings, leaderboards may show real names or pseudonyms
- **Your position**: Your current rank is highlighted

## How Rankings Work

Leaderboard rankings are based on:

1. **Autograder scores**: Your active submission's autograder score
2. **Submission time**: In case of ties, earlier submissions rank higher
3. **Real-time updates**: Rankings update automatically as students submit new code

## Leaderboard Privacy

Instructors can configure leaderboard privacy settings:

- **Public leaderboards**: All students can see everyone's rankings
- **Anonymous leaderboards**: Students see rankings but not identities
- **Personal view only**: You only see your own rank and score

## Competitive Assignments

Leaderboards are particularly useful for:

- **Optimization challenges**: Assignments where students compete for the fastest or most efficient solution
- **Performance tasks**: Problems where there are multiple correct approaches with varying quality
- **Extra credit opportunities**: Bonus points for top performers

<Tip>
Focus on learning and understanding the material first. Leaderboards are meant to be fun and motivating, not stressful!
</Tip>

## Opting Out

If you prefer not to participate in leaderboards, check with your instructor about opt-out options. Some courses allow students to hide their names from public leaderboards while still tracking their own progress.
