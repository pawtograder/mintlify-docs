# Forming Groups

## Overview

Pawtograder supports two kinds of group assignments:

- **Group optional**: students can choose to work alone, or form a group.
- **Group required**: students must form a group.

When forming groups, instructors can choose to allow students to form groups themselves, or to form groups for them. This page provides instructions for students to form groups themselves.

Pawtograder uses an invitation system for group formation: students can create new groups, invite other students to join, and accept or reject invitations. Pawtograder automatically sends invitation emails to students who are invited to join a group.

If group submissions are permitted, you will see a clear button to create a new group on the assignment page. If group submissions are optional, and you already have an individual submission, joining a group will automatically deactivate any existing submission or repository.

### Creating a New Group

Click "Create a new group" to create a new group. Pick a good name: other students will use this name to find your group, and instructors will use this name to identify the group. The name must consist only of alphanumeric, hyphens, or underscores, and be less than 36 characters.

Optionally, you can invite other students to join your group right as you create it. They will be notified by email, and can accept or reject the invitation.

After creating the group, you can still invite more students to it later.

### Managing Group Invitations

Once you are in a group, you will see a list of all the students in the group. If your instructor has assigned a mentor to your group, you will see the mentor's name displayed. You can invite more students to join the group or view the status of pending invitations by clicking "Invite other students".

If your instructor has assigned a mentor to your group, you will see the mentor's name displayed in the group information.

If your group has an assigned mentor, you will see their name displayed in the group details. Mentors are assigned by instructors and may be responsible for grading your group's submissions.

### Joining a Group

To join a group or accept an invitation, click "Join a group" on the assignment page.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/students/assignments/form-groups-1754511323362.png" alt="Join a group button on the assignment page" />
</Frame>

You can see any groups that you have been invited to and accept or decline the invitation. You can also select any other group that you would like to join to send a request (which must be approved by any existing group member).

## Exporting Group Information

Instructors can export group information to CSV format for record-keeping and analysis. The export includes:

- **Group Name**: The name of each group
- **Student Names**: All students in the group, formatted as "Name <email>"
- **Mentor Name**: The assigned mentor (if applicable), formatted as "Name <email>"

The CSV export uses UTF-8 encoding with BOM for compatibility with Excel and includes formula-safe escaping to prevent security issues when opening in spreadsheet applications. Rows are sorted alphabetically by group name for consistent exports.

## Group Notifications

Students receive notifications about group activities at appropriate times:

- **Invitation emails**: When you're invited to join a group, you'll receive an email notification
- **Assignment release**: Group notifications are only sent after an assignment is released to students
- **No premature notifications**: You won't receive notifications about groups for unreleased assignments, even if instructors are setting up groups in advance

This ensures you only receive relevant notifications about assignments you can currently access.
