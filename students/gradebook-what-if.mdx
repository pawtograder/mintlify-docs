# What-if grade simulations

The gradebook what-if feature lets you simulate hypothetical grades to see how they would affect your calculated grades and final course grade.

## Enabling what-if

Your instructor controls whether what-if simulations are enabled for your course. If enabled, you can tap on released grades to enter hypothetical values.

When what-if is disabled, the gradebook is view-only and shows a notice at the top.

## Using what-if

### Simulate a grade

1. Navigate to your gradebook
2. Tap on any released grade card
3. Enter a hypothetical score
4. Press Enter or tap outside the input

The grade card will show your simulated value with a magic wand icon. Any calculated columns that depend on this grade will automatically update to reflect your simulation.

### Clear a simulation

To return to your actual grade:

1. Tap on the simulated grade card
2. Clear the input field (delete all text)
3. Press Enter

All dependent calculated columns will return to their actual values.

## What you can simulate

- **Manual columns**: Participation, attendance, or other instructor-entered grades
- **Assignment columns**: Grades linked to programming assignments
- **Released columns only**: You can only simulate grades that your instructor has released

## What you cannot simulate

- **Calculated columns**: Columns that use expressions (like averages or totals) are read-only. They update automatically based on your simulations of their source columns.
- **Unreleased columns**: Grades that haven't been released by your instructor

## How calculations work

When you simulate a grade, Pawtograder recalculates all dependent columns using the same expressions your instructor defined. For example:

- If your final grade is `mean(gradebook_columns("hw-*")) * 0.7 + gradebook_columns("participation") * 0.3`
- And you simulate a higher participation score
- Your final grade will update to show what you would earn with that participation score

The calculations respect the same rules as actual grades:
- Missing grades count as 0 unless excused
- Droppable grades can be dropped by `drop_lowest()`
- Weighted averages account for different maximum scores

## Tips

- Use what-if to understand how much an upcoming assignment affects your grade
- Simulate different scenarios to plan your study priorities
- Remember that simulations are local to your browser and don't affect your actual grades
- Your instructor cannot see your what-if simulations
